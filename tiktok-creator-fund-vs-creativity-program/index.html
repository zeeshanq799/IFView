What Was the TikTok Creator Fund?

The TikTok Creator Fund was TikTok's original monetization program launched in 2020. It paid creators based on video views, engagement, and authenticity, with typical earnings of $0.02-$0.05 per 1,000 views.

Why it's being discontinued: The Creator Fund faced criticism for extremely low payouts. Creators with millions of views often earned less than $100 per month. TikTok recognized this wasn't competitive with YouTube or Instagram monetization, leading to the development of the Creativity Program.

Phase-out timeline: TikTok began transitioning creators to Creativity Program in 2024 and plans complete Creator Fund discontinuation by Q4 2026.

What Is the Creativity Program?

The Creativity Program (officially "TikTok Creativity Program Beta") is TikTok's new monetization system that pays 10-20x more than the Creator Fund for qualified content.

‚úÖ Creativity Program Requirements

  • 10,000 followers minimum
  • 100,000 video views in the past 30 days
  • 18+ years old
  • Account in good standing (no violations)
  • Create videos over 1 minute long
  • Original content only (no reposts or stolen content)

Key difference: Only videos longer than 1 minute qualify for Creativity Program earnings. This incentivizes longer-form content that generates higher engagement and ad revenue.

Why Qualified Views Matter

Not all views count toward Creativity Program earnings. Only "qualified views" generate revenue:

What Qualifies?

  • Video length: Must be over 1 minute (60+ seconds)
  • Originality: No reposted or duplicate content
  • Authenticity: Real engagement, no botted views
  • Guidelines compliance: No community guideline violations

Which Program Should You Choose?

The decision is straightforward for most creators:

Choose Creativity Program If:

  • You meet the requirements (10K followers, 100K monthly views)
  • You can create videos longer than 1 minute consistently
  • Your niche allows for longer-form storytelling or tutorials
  • You want to maximize earnings per view

Stay on Creator Fund Only If:

  • You don't meet Creativity Program requirements yet
  • Your content format requires videos under 60 seconds
  • You're focused on Shorts/Reels-style rapid content

Reality check: Given that Creativity Program pays 10-20x more, most creators should transition as soon as they meet requirements. Even if only 50% of your views qualify, you'll likely earn more than 100% Creator Fund views.

How to Maximize Creativity Program Earnings

Optimize your content strategy for maximum qualified views and RPM:

1. Video Length Optimization

Sweet spot: 1.5-2.5 minutes
Long enough to qualify but short enough to maintain retention. TikTok rewards high retention rates with better distribution.

2. Target Tier 1 Audiences

Create content appealing to US, UK, Canada, and Australia viewers. Use English language, discuss topics relevant to these markets, and post during their peak hours (7-10 PM local time).

3. Focus on High-CPM Niches

Niches with strong advertiser demand generate higher RPM:

  • Finance & investing: $0.80-$1.50 RPM
  • Tech & productivity: $0.60-$1.20 RPM
  • Business & entrepreneurship: $0.50-$1.00 RPM
  • Health & fitness: $0.40-$0.80 RPM
  • Entertainment & comedy: $0.20-$0.50 RPM

4. Maintain High Engagement

TikTok considers watch time, likes, comments, and shares when determining qualified views. Videos with 80%+ retention and high engagement get preferential distribution and better RPM.

5. Batch Long-Form Content

Instead of mixing short (<60s) and long (>60s) videos, batch your content strategy. Post 4-6 longer videos per week rather than mixing with short-form. This maximizes your qualified view percentage.

Common Creativity Program Mistakes

Avoid these errors that reduce earnings:

  • Making 61-second videos: Barely qualifying videos often have lower retention. Aim for 90+ seconds minimum.
  • Padding content: Adding unnecessary filler to reach 1 minute hurts retention and algorithm ranking.
  • Reposting content: Duplicate or stolen content gets flagged, disqualifying views from revenue.
  • Ignoring analytics: Check TikTok Studio to see your qualified view percentage and RPM. Adjust strategy based on data.
  • Mixing short and long: Unless you're building audience first, focus entirely on 1+ minute content to maximize qualified views.
